Interpretation tips:
  • High Ownership (2.40): good coaching entry point for leadership potential, self-advocacy and proactive communication. Link to team roles and future leadership pathways.
  • Elevated Over-Control (1.80): explore perfectionism, fear of asking for help, and burnout risk. Help the client practise delegating and negotiating workload instead of carrying everything alone.
  • Lower Passive (0.90) and Avoidant (0.70): still present, but not dominant. Useful to normalise that everyone sometimes avoids or waits, and then build small “speak up” and “start anyway” habits.

Suggested Coaching Flow (±45 minutes)

  1. Warm-up: favourite subjects, hobbies, “dream jobs”, current stresses. Link early to I- and S-themes (curiosity, people-helping).
  2. RIASEC reflection: unpack ISA – where does the client already see Investigative, Social and Artistic energy in their life?
  3. NIPP scan: highlight strong ORG and DIM as strengths; explore HYP, BURN and LACK as risk-zones for overload and scarcity thinking.
  4. Scenario patterns: affirm Ownership as a strength; gently explore Over-control (“where do you carry too much alone?”) and build experiments in asking for help earlier.
  5. Career clusters: explore top fields (science/data, helping/education, creative/media) with concrete local options, study paths and job-shadow ideas.
  6. Action plan: agree on 3–5 practical steps (research one course, speak to one professional, try one small behaviour change in class or work).
